Have you been summoned to court or do you need help with an ongoing court case?

Many people find the court process to be confusing and stressful. The consequences of a conviction, disqualification or sentence can be quite serious having significant consequences on your life, liberty, family and career.

You need professional and knowledgeable legal advice, quality preparation of your legal documents, research, submissions, affidavits and legal representation in court and someone who cares about the outcome of your case.

Contact Karen Harding Law Barristers & Solicitors and her helpful support team to discuss your case.

You will feel reassured to know that your case is being handled by an experienced lawyer who cares about you, who is knowledgeable about the law and the court process. Karen and her capable support team team will help you to get the right outcome, will guide you through the court process and will assist you with accessing help for any rehabilitative needs.

About Karen

Karen Harding is a Barrister and Solicitor, who is the Principal of her firm and the Trust Account Supervisor who specialises in Criminal and Traffic Law. Her clients include people who have been, or will be, charged with a criminal or traffic offence who need to appear in court or have a driver's licencing problems with NZTA or require a limited licence, Alcohol Interlock licence or require legal submissions to be prepared and require legal advice and representation in court and for parole applications.

Karen has been practicing since 1997 and has represented thousands of clients on charges ranging from driving offences to very serious criminal offences including complex multi-accused jury trials including fraud cases, serious drug offending and sexual crimes and has prepared over a thousand limited licence applications.

Karen is an experienced litigator conducting cases in the District Court, High Court and appeals to highest level including appearing in the Supreme Court.

Since 2015 as well as criminal defence work Karen is also involved in leading, preparing and conducting complex Civil Litigation assisting human trafficking victims, fishers and seafarers to obtain Justice in relation to Human Rights Abuses at sea, unpaid wage claims and Employment Law, Admiralty Law and Fisheries Act matters. This is involved appearing in the Employment Relations Authority (ERA), the Employment Court, the District Court, the High Court, the Admiralty Court, the Court of Appeal and the Supreme Court of New Zealand. This included appearing as counsel in court cases in Christchurch and Wellington and involved international clients, extensive preparation of legal documentation and submissions and involved national and international travel and managing a large team of support staff including Indonesian translators.
